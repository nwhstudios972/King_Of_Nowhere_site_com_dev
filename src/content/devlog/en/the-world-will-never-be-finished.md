## The original bet

When we started imagining King Of Nowhere, we asked a simple question: why do all MMOs feel like museums? Frozen sets, where players walk through as visitors. We wanted the opposite: a **living**, **modifiable** world that belongs to those who inhabit it.

## No zone is untouchable

In King Of Nowhere, there is no "official map". No protected districts. No scripted capitals. Everything is buildable — so everything is destructible, everything is negotiable, everything is conquered or defended.

You want to raise a temple on a cliff top? Do it. Your rivals burn it down a week later? Build higher.

## Every server will have its own history

Because every stone is laid by a player, every server becomes a unique story. The ruins your children will walk through are the ones your friends left behind. That's the bet.
