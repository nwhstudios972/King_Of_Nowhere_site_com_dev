## No one ever built a kingdom alone

You can cross King Of Nowhere as a solitary soul. That's a valid choice. But the world was designed for those who want to **found something together** — a guild, an order, a dynasty.

Inviting your friends is more than an "add to party" button. It's sharing a banner, a territory, a treasury, shared oaths.

## A guild is a territory

Every guild can claim a parcel of the server. Raise walls there, workshops, temples. Recruit NPCs, welcome other players, open or close their gates.

Want to set up a neutral trading post at the crossing of two roads? A fortress on a ridge top? A cathedral forbidden to mortals? Your friends lay the stones with you.

## Real co-op play

- **Build**: shared construction sites, pooled resources, blueprints drafted by many hands.
- **Fight**: coordinated raids, territory defense, faction wars.
- **Govern**: internal roles, hierarchies you define, guild laws the engine enforces.

Nothing is imposed. You decide whether your guild is a family of five, or a nation of five hundred.

## And the server will remember

When your guild falls, its ruins remain. When it triumphs, its crest marks the map. Guilds aren't menus — they are actors in the world.

See you very soon for the alliance system between guilds.
