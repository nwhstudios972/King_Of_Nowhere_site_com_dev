> *He longs for company, for a place where he will not be feared — but his mere presence remains a curse he cannot escape.*

Born of a forbidden union, Faël carries **a divine light he never asked for** — and the shadow draws him in inevitably.

He seeks neither power, nor to harm. And yet, wherever he passes, **whispers rise… the darkness awakens… and disaster follows him in silence**.

*The unlucky one. The Reluctant Herald.*

## The unstable combination

Faël was born, **fourth and last child** of [Rose](/en/univers/personnages/rose/) and the [Split King](/en/univers/personnages/roi-fendu/) — half-demon, half-human, with **a weakened archangel blood** barely running in his veins.

**An unstable, unpredictable combination.**

Unlike his brothers and sisters who mastered their powers with grace or intent, Faël was **a raw, uncontrolled force** — his hands constantly discharging a wild archangelic energy he could neither contain nor understand.

## Hands in his pockets

He quickly learned to keep his hands buried in his pockets. A habit that became second nature.

But even that was not enough to stop **the disasters that followed him everywhere** like a cursed shadow.

## Innocence beneath the curse

Despite this unwilling curse, Faël was **the most innocent and the gentlest** of his brothers and sisters — a naive smile and a natural kindness that made everyone love him at first sight.

But the moment people saw him coming, **joy turned to apprehension**. Cities prepared for catastrophes. Armies took up defensive positions. For they knew that **destruction would inevitably follow**.

## The journey without welcome

He travelled across the world all the same, desperately seeking his place — trying to understand why he was condemned to be a messenger of misfortune **when all he asked for was love and acceptance**.

People reject him not out of malice, but out of **mere survival**. And Faël carried this loneliness with **a childlike resignation** that broke hearts.

## The power he cannot see

And that is perhaps **Faël's greatest power** — not the destruction he causes, but **the guilt he inspires** in those who flee him.

For one cannot hate someone so innocent.

## A gentle tragedy

His story is one of **a gentle tragedy** — of a cursed child who does not understand why the world fears him, and who keeps walking forward all the same.

Hands in his pockets. **Disaster trailing behind him.**
