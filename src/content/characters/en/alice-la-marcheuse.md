> *"I am never early. The world is late."*

No one knows where Alice comes from. No one has ever seen her leave. And yet, wherever **an event is about to take shape**, she is already there — light luggage, a walker's smile, **her stall ready**.

## The walker who knows

Alice appears before **weddings**, before **coronations**, before **battles**. Before the things people believe to be secret. She sets up her stand, takes her crates from her bag, and waits.

Always early. Always exactly the right spot. As if **the world were whispering its own appointments to her**.

People sometimes ask her. She shrugs with a walker's smile:

> *"I am never early. The world is late."*

## The mascots of the road

On her cart, in her arms, on her shoulders, in the folds of her cloak — **creatures**.

Small. Strange. Affectionate. **Mascots.**

Foxes with eyes too calm. Birds that seem to understand spoken words. Cats that never sleep. Lizards with warm scales.

She does not really sell them — she **presents them**. And if a mascot accepts a new human, it goes off with them. **Otherwise it stays with Alice.**

> *"It is not me who chooses. It never is."*

## The items that turn a war

Most of what she sells is modest — trinkets, vials, scarves, charms.

But sometimes — **rarely** — Alice pulls an item from her inner pocket. A blade whose story alone is enough to make kings tremble. A scroll no one should be able to read. A ring that remembers.

On that day, **a war will change sides**.

She never says which item, nor when. She only takes it out when she has decided that **the moment is right**. And she never accepts gold as payment for those items. **The price is something else.**

## What she might be

Some think Alice is a seer who never says what she sees. Others, that she is older than she appears. A few claim to have seen her in tales too ancient for her to have lived them.

Her — she walks. She sells. She smiles.

And she keeps walking, mascots on her shoulders, toward **the next pivotal moment** no one has yet seen coming.
