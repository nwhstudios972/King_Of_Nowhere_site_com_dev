> *"I'm not done serving. Come back later to die."*

Mother Ossuelle has gray, taut skin, eyes hollowed by two warm embers, and a rag **always** in her left hand. She no longer quite has fingers — more like bones polished by six hundred years of wiping the counter. She speaks to customers like to children she has known when they were knee-high. Every customer.

## A death she refused

In life, Ossuelle was human. She ran **The Ash Vigil** on a pilgrimage road between two human holy cities. A basic inn, bone soup, clean beds, honest prices. She kept it forty years. She was never rich. She didn't care.

She died one winter evening, **behind her counter, a rag in her hand**, wiping a wine stain. Heart attack. Sixty-two years old. A clean, banal, ridiculous death.

Except Ossuelle refused.

She saw herself leave. She watched her body fall. And the first thing she thought was: *"And the soup? There's still three pilgrims sleeping upstairs, who'll serve them the soup?"*

## The pact with the Entity

It is rare. It is *very* rare. But the Entity listens, sometimes.

It appeared in the counter — not before, *in* — as a long, cold current. It asked a simple question:

> *"Do you wish to stay?"*

Ossuelle answered without hesitation: *"As long as there are pilgrims, I serve."*

The pact was sealed immediately, with **one condition only**: never close the inn. Not an hour. Not a minute. If the door closes even once, Ossuelle leaves — really, this time.

**She has not closed in six hundred years.**

## Character

Maternal, brusque, fatally direct. Calls everyone *"darling"* or *"sweetheart"*, even kings, even major demons. Insults those who refuse to eat their soup. Believes wholeheartedly that **no one thinks well on an empty stomach**.

Keeps a large leather-bound ledger where she writes down every customer's name. Not for the bill — to remember. She has six hundred years of names.

Despised by most other undead, who find it obscene that one of theirs **protects the living**. She does not care in the slightest. *"My pilgrims are my pilgrims. The other undead can go sulk."*

## Exclusive ability — *Ossuelle's Candle*

When a customer becomes a regular, Ossuelle can, without telling them, **light a candle in their name** in the back room. The candles line a whole wall — thousands of them.

As long as a player's candle burns:
- If that player dies **in the same region as the inn**, their soul is automatically **called back to The Ash Vigil**, instead of being lost or rerouted. They wake up on a straw mattress upstairs, alive, weak, but alive.
- The candle melts a little with each recall. A candle saves **only once**. Then it goes out.
- **One candle per player, never two.** Ossuelle is strict about this.

She does not light a candle for just anyone. You must have eaten at her place at least **three times**. And finished your plate.

## Sanctuary

- **Indestructible**: sealed by the direct pact with the Entity. Even a major demon cannot enter if it carries intent to harm — the threshold **burns its feet** like salt on a wound.
- The inn **cannot close**. The shutters stay open. The door has no lock. If someone tries to barricade it by force, the planks **fall off on their own**.
- No undead can enter unless *personally invited* by Ossuelle. The pact makes her superior in authority to other undead — she is, literally, **the Entity's only employee in this line of work**.

> *"Eat your soup, darling. We'll talk after. Or not. But first, eat."*
