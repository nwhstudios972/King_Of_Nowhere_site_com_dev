> *"I obeyed for three thousand years. Today, I pour tea. It is harder."*

Lior pours the boiling water at exactly the right height, without looking. She hears the heat rising in the porcelain and stops her hand at precisely the right moment. Her wings — folded against her back — are veiled with a soft gray, as if covered by fine ash.

That gray is her mark. It is what tells, to anyone who can read angels, that she has **chosen**.

## The sentence she refused to sign

Lior sat on the Council of Feathers of the Celestial Palace. Three thousand years of service. A respected voice, never cutting, never cowardly.

One day, **Marie** laid before her a collective sentence: erase a demonic city that sheltered — among others — families, children, unarmed civilians. The military logic was clean. The target was legitimate. The cost was acceptable, *by angelic standards*.

Lior set down the quill. She looked at Marie. She said:

> *"If I sign this, I am no longer an angel. I am an accountant."*

And she left.

## The silent miracle

**Marie let her go without stripping her wings.** No one, in the history of the Palace, has ever left the Council of Feathers without having their wings torn off on the way out.

Lior walked to the frontier. She laid her first stone in a valley that neither angels nor demons claimed. She built **The Veil of Wings** — a low inn, a wide roof, windows that look in every direction.

Lior's former colleagues call her a traitor. The demons don't know what to make of her. Marie has never said a word about her publicly.

But when an angelic traveler comes knocking at her door, weeping, lost, broken — they are always welcomed. As if **someone, up there, knew where to send them**.

## Character

Gentle, but cutting when she must be. Refuses compliments. Never gives unsolicited advice. Pours tea at exactly the right temperature without ever asking the customer's preference — and she is always right.

She will house a demon **if it lays down its weapon at the door and lowers its eyes for three seconds**. No more. No less. The ritual is dry, almost administrative.

She never speaks of the Palace. If someone tries to fish for information about angels, she smiles politely and brings the bill.

## Exclusive ability — *Threshold Blessing*

When a customer leaves the inn, Lior can — with a single glance — **place an invisible seal on their forehead**. The seal lasts until the next IRL sunset.

Effect: **the first mortal blow taken outside is reduced to 1 HP remaining**. Once only. The seal then burns, visible for a second on the player's forehead, then vanishes.

Lior can place **3 seals per cycle** (24h IRL). She chooses who. She never explains.

Demons can receive the seal too. That is what most scandalizes the other angels.

## Sanctuary

- **Indestructible** as long as the **feathers embedded** in the inn's walls remain in place. Lior planted one at every wood joint. They have never lost their light.
- No attack, no spell, no intent to harm can take shape under the roof — whoever tries feels their own wings (real or metaphorical) fold against themselves.
- Demons who enter without having laid down their weapon are **pushed back at the threshold** as by a calm wind. Not wounded. Just prevented.

> *"The Veil is not a side. It is a pause. When you leave, you choose."*
