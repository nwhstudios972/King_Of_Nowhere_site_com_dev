> *Mother of a king torn between two truths,*
> *Marie bore the burden of the impossible choice.*
> *Between duty and love, between heaven and abyss,*
> *she raised her son in a world*
> *that could not understand him.*

> *She did not choose the Split King.*
> *But she chose to never stop loving him.*

## The equal of Belzebut

Marie was **the supreme archangel**, equal in power to **Belzebut** — but different. She was guided by **compassion**, not ambition.

When Belzebut fell and was transformed into a demon, she was one of the few who did not condemn him. Seeing **beyond his corruption** the tragedy of his fall, she kept in her heart the love for who he had been. And that love allowed her to see **light even within shadow**.

## The impossible love

She dared to approach Belzebut and, against all odds, he answered her tenderness — for in her he found an echo of what he had lost.

Together they had a child. **An impossible blend of the divine and the abomination**, a creature neither fully light nor fully dark.

## The birth of the Split King

The **Split King** was born this way, bearing a duality that would condemn him never to be accepted by either world.

Marie became **the protective mother**, trying to guide her child through a universe that rejected him — convinced that love might be the only force able to save him.

She stayed at Belzebut's side despite his transformation, believing that **even a demon deserved to be loved**.

## The Gate of the Core

Yet her love was not enough to stop the wars to come, nor to save those she cherished. Marie learned that compassion, however noble, **cannot always triumph over the chaos others create**.

So she made her decision: she would seal **the Gate to the Core of the Entity** herself, so that no one would ever again fall as Belzebut had. No one — **except the most worthy**.

That is where she stands to this day. Upright. On guard. In love.
