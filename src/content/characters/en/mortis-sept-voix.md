> *"We are seven. How many do you hear when I speak? Seven? Good. You're honest. Most hear one and lie."*

Mortis is tall, lean, and still wears **the armor** he died in at the First March — split at the right shoulder, where Vargoth finished him. Under the skull-black helm, his face is **gray like washed stone**. His eyes burn softly, **two white flames** that do not blink.

When he speaks, **seven voices come out at once** from under the helm — his own, and those of the six men who died under his command on the same day. Not a choir — a layering. Strange. Unsettling. Magnificent.

## The man who would not be done

Mortis was a **human general** under [Galen Iron-Rim's](/en/univers/personnages/galen-bord-de-fer/) command at the defense of the First March — not a superior, a peer. Six men under his direct command. When [Vargoth](/en/univers/personnages/vargoth-couronne-de-suie/) broke the line, they fell together.

Mortis was finished last. **Vargoth looked at him.** Mortis smiled (he says he remembers). Vargoth struck.

Mortis should have left like the others. He refused — not out of pride, out of debt. His six men had died listening to him. *He owed them something.*

## Welcomed by Mother Ossuelle

He rose three days later. He walked to **The Ash Vigil**. He knocked on the door (which never closes — he just knocked to signal). Mother Ossuelle looked at him. She said *"sit, darling"*.

She kept him **seven years**. She taught him **how not to become corrupted** — how to carry his dead without feeding on them, how to honor their memory without warping it. She is the one who coined the name "Seven-Voices" — *"you're not alone anymore, darling. Six with you. Seven in all. Carry them well."*

After seven years, Mortis left. He kissed Mother Ossuelle on the forehead (she grumbled), and he walked back into his march.

## The work of souls

Mortis has a mission he gave himself alone: **gather wandering souls** from battlefields before demonic necromancers take them to make corrupted undead slaves.

He escorts these souls — according to their wishes — either to Mother Ossuelle (repentant humans), to Lior's Veil (wounded angels), or to the Bridge frontier (the undecided). He does not judge them. He asks where they want to go. *"It's your choice, soldier. Not mine."*

Marie herself recognized him publicly as a **"partially allied undead"**. A category that didn't exist — created for him.

## Character

Grave, measured, **deeply polite**. Always greets with a slight bow of the head. Refuses gratuitous violence. Fights only demons and necromancers — never neutral living, never angels.

Loves singing deeply. When he sings in seven voices, **soldiers kneel — even among angels** — not from devotion, from mystical respect. Vargoth, for his part, **covers his ears** when he hears Mortis sing. It is the only known weakness Vargoth has.

For neutral guilds, **escorting Mortis** during a soul transfer is an honor mission. For demonic guilds (especially necromancers), **bringing him down** is a priority revenge — he steals too many souls from them.

## Spawn and movement

- **Wandering boss** on old battlefields — appears where recent dead have not been gathered.
- **Gives escort quests**: protect a convoy of wandering souls to The Ash Vigil or The Veil of Wings.
- **Appears on event** when a massive number of players die in the same spot (10+ deaths in 5 minutes). He comes to harvest. Necromancers come too.

## Epic weapon drop — *Skull-Helm of the Seven*

Helm-weapon. **A fused skull** with six small skulls welded around it, forming a grinning crown. Held in hand, swung, or worn as a helm.

- **Inflicts a "Doubt" debuff on struck enemies**: -30% damage for 10 seconds. Enemies hear the seven voices in their head and hesitate.
- Worn as a helm, **grants immunity to charm and fear**.
- **Restriction**: a major demon wearing it hears the skull **sing to itself** constantly — unbearable distraction, -50% spell focus.

## Epic skill drop — *Song of the Seven*

The wielder sings (mechanic: vocal gauge). **Seven allied specters** appear at their side for **60 seconds**.

- The specters target **only demons** within 80m.
- They pass through walls and ignore normal armor.
- They sing while fighting — a layered sound that inflicts **-20% damage** on every demon who hears them (stacks with the "Doubt" debuff).
- **Two uses per IRL day.** *"They are tired too. Seven is a weight."*
