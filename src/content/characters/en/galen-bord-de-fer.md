> *"I buried my company here. I built the house on top. Nobody dies twice under my roof."*

Galen has nothing of a hero. He has a rough voice, broad hands, an apron burned in the same places for twenty years. He wipes the counter like a man who has wiped too much blood — methodically, without thinking, looking elsewhere.

## The sergeant who couldn't die

Galen survived three sieges during the war of the Breaches. Sergeant of the **First March**, he ran a field kitchen while demons reduced his company to names carved on wooden plaques.

He says he lost more friends than he has served bowls of soup. And he has served many.

When the war stopped — not ended, just stopped — Galen refused to go home. He had no home anymore. He had a field, a mass grave, and a debt he could not repay.

He built his inn directly on top of the pit. **The Crooked Lantern** grew there, on the earth that holds his brothers.

## The counter's oath

Beneath Galen's counter, carved into the wood with a knife, **the names of every dead soldier of his company**. Forty-seven names. He rereads them every morning before opening.

That is what protects the inn.

The pact is not written, not magical in the sense that mages mean it. It is simpler, and harder to break: Galen swore before the dead that **no one would ever die under this roof again**. And the dead, they listen.

As long as a single name remains legible beneath the counter, the inn holds. No weapon strikes home inside. No flame spreads. The walls do not fall.

## Character

Gruff, sparing with words, looks everyone in the eye — kings most of all.

He refuses to extend credit to the powerful. He feeds lost children and soldiers who don't know where to go home free of charge. He likes neither angels, nor demons, nor fine speeches. He likes when the plate is empty at the end of the meal.

He never tells his war stories. If someone else tells them for him, he pours a round and changes the subject.

## Exclusive ability — *The Sergeant's Toast*

Once per cycle (24h IRL), Galen can offer **a round to the whole room**. The drinkers become **immune to fear** for 1h IRL in open world — total resistance to panic, terror, and forced-flight effects, including those inflicted by legendary bosses.

The toast comes at a cost: when the effect ends, **a name is carved** into the list under the counter in the drinker's name. If that player betrays another patron of the inn within the next 30 days, Galen closes his door to them **for life**.

## Sanctuary

- **Indestructible** as long as at least one name remains legible beneath the counter.
- Any weapon drawn inside drops to the floor — Galen places it himself near the exit.
- Wounded soldiers who cross the threshold recover full HP **passively** as long as they remain seated.

> *"Here, you put down your helmet. You eat. You sleep. Tomorrow, you leave. That's all I offer."*
