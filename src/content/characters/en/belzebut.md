> *"I was light. I have become shadow. But it is not the fall that defines my glory — it is the choice to reign where others trembled."*

## The favoured archangel

In the beginning, Belzebut was the most powerful and the most beloved archangel of the **Entity**, granted boundless power and privileges no king could have enjoyed. He had everything — including **a wife whose powers were rivalled only by her majestic appearance, a formidable warrior**.

But the power he was granted no longer sufficed. He always wanted more, always to transcend the limits placed upon him.

## The descent to the Core

One day, driven by that insatiable thirst, he dared to descend **into the heart of the universe, into the very core of creation**, seeking a hidden power the Entity itself had not given him.

What he found shattered him. A force so absolute that it broke him — him, the mighty, the favourite, reduced to nothing before the infinite.

## The judgement

The Entity looked upon his rebellion with indifference and pronounced its judgement without anger:

> *"You wanted to surpass me for your own ends. You believed the unity of worlds could be touched, or held by a single being. I know your heart. So you shall become ugliness itself, a sliver of my chaos, the antithesis of your former glory."*

## The King of Hell

And so Belzebut was transformed. He became the King of Demons, **founding an entire race of creatures born from his fall and his corruption**. From the radiant archangel emerged a creature of darkness, carrying within him the rage of having tasted the limitless only to be chained.

## The invisible scar

And yet, somewhere within him, **a trace of who he had been still remained** — a longing for the light he had lost forever. That is why the archangel **Marie** stayed by his side, to ease his suffering.

From their union would be born the story's antagonist, **the Split King** ("Le Roi Fendu"), himself cast out by the angels.

And it is that invisible scar that might, one day, make him more dangerous still than all his demonic powers.
