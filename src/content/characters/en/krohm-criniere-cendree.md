> *"You crossed the threshold. You're pack. Don't touch my hearth, I don't touch your throat."*

Krohm laughs loud. Too loud, sometimes. He claps shoulders, serves beer in horn mugs, and wears an ash-colored mane that falls to his shoulders like a living cloak. He smells of burnt wood and metal. He puts the meat directly on the table — no plate, no cutlery. *You eat like a wolf, you live like a wolf.*

## The pack that should not have fallen

Krohm was alpha. A pack of **forty-two**, which is enormous for wolfs — most don't exceed fifteen. A respected pack, even cited among humans. The kind of pack that could hold a valley alone for a whole season.

During the war of the Breaches, they received an order — *an order*, them who obey no one — to hold a pass against a demonic column. They held. Three days. Forty-one died.

Krohm woke under two bodies, his own half torn, **with a major demon finishing him off**. He bit first. The demon fell. Krohm rose.

He howled an entire night. No one howled back. He understood.

## The inn he built from his dead

Krohm refused to die. He also refused to join another pack — not from pride, from loyalty. A pack, you have one.

He stayed in the valley. He buried his brothers, one by one. And he built his inn from **the bones of the demons that had killed them**. Walls of ribs, beams of femurs, counter of jaws. At the center: a hearth dug straight into stone, lit with an ember he carried from the battlefield.

**That hearth has not gone out in forty years.**

## Character

Rough, warm, loud. Claps shoulders, laughs at heavy jokes, serves generously. But he has one rule: *no one insults a customer in front of him*. The insult falls on **the whole pack** — that is, every drinker that night. And Krohm answers for the whole pack.

He despises lone wolves who reject the very idea of pack. He still serves them — that is his code — but he doesn't speak to them. He says a lone wolf is a dead wolf who doesn't know it yet.

He hates major demons — viscerally. He still serves them, because **it is his code**. But the beer is lukewarm. On purpose.

## Exclusive ability — *Hearth Pact*

When a customer leaves the inn, they may, of their own will, **declare themselves "pack-brother" at the hearth**. Krohm does not invite — the player has to ask.

Once the declaration is made:
- Krohm **senses the position** of the pack-brother anywhere on the map, as long as they live.
- If the brother is attacked the same IRL day, Krohm can, **once only**, appear at their side in the form of a **massive spectral wolf** for 60 seconds. He fights. He does not speak. He vanishes in smoke.
- The pact expires at the next IRL sunset.

Limit: Krohm can hold **only 5 simultaneous pacts**. If a 6th person asks, he refuses — the pack is full.

## Sanctuary

- **Indestructible** as long as the central hearth burns. The original ember is still there, at the heart of the blaze. No one knows how it lasts.
- No weapon can be drawn inside — whoever tries feels their own teeth grow in their mouth. A warning from the ghost pack.
- Major demons **cannot cross the threshold**. Minor demons can — but they pay double for their beer.

> *"My brothers are in the walls. My hearth is in the stone. My pack stopped at forty-one — unless you choose to be the forty-second."*
