> *"We lost once. Once. The next time, we'll be the ones carving the names on the plaques."*

Vargoth wears a twisted crown of **compressed soot** on his head — not a trophy, **a scar**. During the battle of the First March, his own horde burned around him, and the ash fused to his skull and never came off. He says he keeps it **so as not to forget**.

His skin is dark red, his horns asymmetrical (one snapped mid-combat), and his arms are **engraved** with the names of the demons who died at the First March. Three hundred names. He recites the list before every battle.

## The defeat that defines him

During the war of the Breaches, Vargoth led **a horde of four hundred demons** against a human company defending a pass: the **First March**, commanded by a certain sergeant — a human like any other, but one who would not fall.

Three days. Four hundred demons. **Forty-seven humans dead**, but the pass held.

The human sergeant survived. Vargoth's horde did not. Vargoth survived by accident: a demonic corpse fell on him and hid him from the Angels combing the field afterward.

The sergeant? It was [Galen Iron-Rim](/en/univers/personnages/galen-bord-de-fer/).

Vargoth has never forgotten his name.

## The return to the crown

Belzébut reinstated him to command, against the advice of the demonic council. *"One defeat is a lesson. Two is a flaw. You're only at one, Vargoth. Get up."*

That day, Vargoth swore **to reopen GrandPa's Scar** and lead the legion **onto the Bridge of Worlds** to break what is left of the angelic defense. And his first stop — the one he keeps secret — will be Galen's inn.

## The harangue

Vargoth is **the best orator in Draktar**. His pre-battle speeches last twenty minutes. Not one too many. Not one fewer. Demons who listen to the whole thing gain a **temporary buff** (game mechanic: +25% damage for 1h IRL).

Those who leave before the end of the harangue? He burns them himself, in front of the others. That is the rule. No one has complained in a long time.

## Character

Burning, theatrical, **precise**. Knows how to play grandeur when he must, and cutting brevity when it's better. Despises demons who scheme in the shadows (a pointed look at [Sahari](/en/univers/personnages/sahari-tisseuse-d-ombres/)).

Loves being followed. Adores demonic guilds that organize around his name. **Refuses guilds that boast without having proven anything.**

Demonic guilds fight to **march with him** on the Bridge of Worlds — the title of "Soot-Marcher" is one of the most coveted on the demon side. Angelic guilds see him as **the sword of the next war**.

## Spawn and movement

- **World boss** on the ruins of Draktar — always accessible, always dangerous.
- **Periodic march toward the Bridge of Worlds** (1x per IRL month). During the march, he is joined by every demon player who wants to follow. This is **the monthly siege event**: Vargoth + horde vs Seraphiel + angel defenders.
- **Moves on event** to defend a besieged demonic city.

## Epic weapon drop — *Crown-Axe*

Massive two-handed axe forged from **a major demon's rib** (his fallen lieutenant at the First March). Heavy, hideous, magnificent.

- **+50% damage against named angels** (NPCs or players with an angelic title).
- When the wielder kills an angel, **the axe howls** — audible at 200m. Every demon who hears it gains +10% speed for 30 seconds.
- **Restriction**: an uncorrupted human who touches it loses 10% HP per second while holding it.

## Epic skill drop — *March of the Breaches*

The wielder lets out a cry that carries 100m. For **30 seconds**:

- **Every allied demon** in range gains **+50% movement speed** and **total immunity to fear**.
- Demons recently killed in the zone rise as **ephemeral skeletons** and fight for 15 seconds before falling again.
- **3 uses per IRL day.** Vargoth, of course, abuses his own.

> *"Galen is still alive. So am I. As long as one of us breathes, the war is not over."*
