> *"The Code is not cruel. The Code is clear. It is interpretation that wounds. Mine cuts clean."*

Aelinor has the eyes of a trial-reader, the hands of a calligrapher, and a golden quill that never leaves her belt. Her wings are bone-white, and **still** most of the time — she almost never flies. She walks. She wants to be seen walking.

When she enters an angelic city, **conversations stop** without her having to ask.

## The absence that gnaws

Aelinor sat on the Council of Feathers beside [Lior](/en/univers/personnages/lior-cieux-voiles/). Three centuries together. She admired her — she never said it aloud, but she admired her.

When Lior set down the quill and left without signing, Aelinor was the **first to demand her arrest for desertion**. She, more than anyone, wanted to judge her.

Marie refused. **Publicly.** *"Lior left of her own will. The Code does not apply to those who choose. Aelinor, you will not touch her."*

It became a scar. Aelinor obeys Marie — she always obeys — but the **ban has gnawed at her for fifty years**. She falls back on others. On all the others.

## The golden quill

Aelinor writes every sentence in **golden ink**, on a special parchment prepared by the Palace scribes. A golden sentence from Aelinor **can only be annulled by Marie herself** — and Marie has never done so.

Aelinor reads the sentence aloud before the target. Then she folds the parchment in four. Then she carries it out. The ritual lasts exactly three minutes. She never deviates.

## Character

Precise, measured, glacial. Never raises her voice. Never smiles. Loves the Palace choral music deeply and sings herself — *alone, in the evening, in her cell*.

Orthodox angels revere her as **the living arm of the Code**. Moderate angels fear her. Heretical angels flee her.

Angelic guilds compete for her favor: being **named "Witness of Aelinor"** at a public execution is one of the Palace's highest honors. Many angel players chase this title.

For demonic guilds, she is **the prime target** — taking down Aelinor mid-public-judgment is to break the angelic law itself.

## Spawn and movement

- **Travels between angelic cities** to judge publicly. Her presence is announced 1h IRL in advance by a bell.
- **Appears on event** when an angel player has betrayed their side (helped a demon, blasphemed, deserted a battle). The event lasts 30 min IRL — during which the traitor is marked and either defended by their guild or abandoned.
- **Occasional boss**: if a demonic guild ambushes her during a judgment, she defends alone. She never calls for help.

## Epic weapon drop — *Verdict's Glaive*

A long, slim sword with a golden edge. Forged by Master Hjorr himself, 200 years ago, at Aelinor's exclusive request.

- **Inflicts no damage on the innocent.** The sword *decides* — if the target has committed no fault against the wielder's guild in the last 30 IRL days, the edge **passes through without harm**.
- Against a guilty target, **doubles critical damage**.
- **Restriction**: a major demon wielding it sees their gold turn to ash in their hand.

## Epic skill drop — *Sentence Pronounced*

The wielder publicly marks a target, reading their name aloud. For **1h IRL**:

- **Every other angel on the server sees the target highlighted** with a golden halo on the map.
- Angel attacks against the target gain **+30% damage**.
- The target cannot hide in a sanctuary inn — **except at Lior's**, by Marie's dispensation.
