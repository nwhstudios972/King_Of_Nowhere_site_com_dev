> *They call him monster. He names himself heir.*

Born of the forbidden union between light and shadow, he carries within him **the weight of two worlds**. Torn between innocence and war, he seeks neither pardon nor crown.

**Only truth. And perhaps… redemption.**

## The blessing of the four natures

The Dishevelled King was born, third child of [Rose](/en/univers/personnages/rose/) and the [Split King](/en/univers/personnages/roi-fendu/), blessed by [the Entity](/en/univers/personnages/l-entite/) itself with **the four natures** — half-archangel, half-demon, half-elf, half-human.

**A being of perfect balance in a world of chaos.**

From his earliest years, he understood that his power was immense, almost without limit. But that this power meant nothing **without a noble purpose** — and he chose to serve the good of all rather than his own desires.

## The traveller of kingdoms

He journeyed through the realms — counselling kings, helping peoples to flourish, bringing **wisdom and strength** where doubt and weakness reigned.

He became a figure revered and honoured by all. But behind every counsel given, every life saved, every kingdom set right, **another battle was hidden** — silent, intimate, internal.

## The torment of the nights

His true battle, the one that torments him every night, is **his father** — the [Split King](/en/univers/personnages/roi-fendu/), broken by the loss of [Rose](/en/univers/personnages/rose/), sinking ever deeper into a destructive fury no one can stop.

It is also **his grandfather** — [Belzebut](/en/univers/personnages/belzebut/), the origin of the darkness running through his own veins, an inheritance he carries without ever having chosen it.

## Faith in redemption

The Dishevelled King believes deeply that **everyone deserves a chance at redemption** — that even the darkest of creatures can be saved.

But he doubts, secretly, that it may be possible **for his father**.

He carries within him this existential question — the certainty that one must try, **while knowing perhaps that it is futile**.

He helps others without expecting anything in return, always seeking the solution that minimises suffering and benefits the greatest number. But he constantly asks himself **whether his philosophy can apply to the one who created him**.

## The impossible burden

And perhaps therein lies his greatest tragedy: **to be so powerful and so wise**, and yet **to feel powerless** before the abyss of his own kin.

For the Dishevelled King carries upon his shoulders **the burden of saving a world that could destroy itself**.

And every day, he wonders whether he will succeed.

Or whether he will be forced to do **the unthinkable**.
