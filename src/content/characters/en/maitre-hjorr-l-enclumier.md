> *"You want me to reforge that? Good. First, go find the missing shard. Not now, kid. Before you cry."*

Hjorr is a two-meter-ten giant, bald, **arms entirely covered in black and red runes** that shift slightly while he forges. His beard is braided with silver thread, and around his neck he wears a **chain of sixteen rings** — one for each great hero whose weapon he has reforged.

He laughs loud. Drinks heavily. Hits **even harder**. And works in absolute silence the moment he sets hammer to metal — silence that can last five straight days.

## The smith who refuses kings

Hjorr is sought by every kingdom — humans, angels, demons. He refuses them all. *"I forge for swords that deserve their wielders. Not for wielders who want a sword."*

Three human kings sent envoys with chests of gold. He sent the chests back full, **with a small pencil drawing of a hammer** inside. No one dared send a second envoy.

But when an anonymous hero arrives, wounded, carrying the broken pieces of their father's weapon — Hjorr looks, reads the metal, and says *"sit"*. And three days later, the weapon breathes again.

## Blanche's sword

Sixty years ago, [Blanche](/en/univers/personnages/blanche/) came to Hjorr **with her Entity-blessed sword broken in two**. She had snapped it against [the Heir of the Void](/en/univers/personnages/heritier-du-neant/), in a fight she had walked out of alive but in tears.

Hjorr looked at the sword. He looked at Blanche. He said: *"Go sit down. Eat. Drink. Come back in ten days."*

He reforged it. No one knows how — the Entity's blessing should have been lost. It was not. Blanche never asked why. Hjorr never explained. A silver ring on his neck-chain has been there ever since.

Blanche, for her part, sometimes comes to drink with him. Without speaking. Just drinking.

## The test

When a player asks Hjorr to forge or reforge something, he sets **a test**. Always different. Never easy.

Sometimes it is fetching a shard of ore from a dangerous biome. Sometimes it is bringing back a deserter alive. Sometimes it is just **sitting and listening for three hours** without asking a question.

If he accepts the test, he accepts the work. If he refuses, he refuses **forever** — no point coming back.

## Character

Gruff, generous, rigorous. Loves family stories — always knows which weapon belonged to which grandfather. Refuses gold payment when the weapon has a story.

Has **survived 11 looting attempts** — the last, by a fourteen-demon raid, ended with him delivering fourteen pairs of horns to Vargoth, **wrapped in a clean sheet**. Vargoth did not comment.

For neutral and human guilds, **mounting a quest expedition for Hjorr** is one of the great honors of the game. Angels and demons respect him — no one has dared attack him in force in fifteen years.

## Spawn and movement

- **Sets his mobile anvil** near a human or neutral city. Stays 2 IRL weeks in one place, then moves.
- **Gives reforge quests**: retrieve fragments of mythic weapons scattered across the map (rare boss drops). Multiple quests can run in parallel.
- **Becomes a world boss** if attacked — he fights alone, strikes with the hammer, and never flees. The 12th looting attempt has yet to happen.

## Epic weapon drop — *Hammer of Dawn*

A smith's hammer. Two-handed. Rough iron head set with a single gem of shifting color.

- **Can break a legendary weapon in two hits** (anti-loot mechanic: destroy a fallen player's weapon to prevent enemy recovery).
- **Can reforge a broken weapon in one hit** (the wielder passes over a broken weapon on the ground and restores it in passing).
- **Restriction**: cannot be used to attack a merchant or innkeeper NPC — the head refuses to fall.

## Epic skill drop — *True Reforge*

The wielder restores another player's broken weapon **to 100% power + 10% permanent bonus**.

- **Once per IRL week.** The bonus is engraved on the weapon — visible and lasting.
- The reforged weapon then bears **a small Hjorr rune** on its grip. Recognized everywhere. Respected almost everywhere.

> *"A weapon is a promise. You make it to yourself, or to someone else. If you break it, you come back. If you come back, I look. And if you deserve it, I forge."*
