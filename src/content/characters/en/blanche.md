> *"I choose neither heaven nor darkness. I choose those who need me."*

Born of light and shadow, Blanche watches over the balance between worlds. Neither angel nor demon, she is the bridge between two heritages. **Her compassion is her armour. Her faith, her blade.** Wherever chaos is born, she brings hope.

**She is the Guardian.**

## The radiant child

Blanche was born of the secret love between [Rose](/en/univers/personnages/rose/) and the [Split King](/en/univers/personnages/roi-fendu/) — half-angel, half-elf.

But **unlike her brothers**, she inherited **only the angelic light and the elven grace** — without a single trace of demonic darkness.

From childhood she was the radiant one, the child who naturally shone on the side of good, carrying within her **a purity that even her parents could not quite understand**.

## The blessing of the Entity

[The Entity](/en/univers/personnages/l-entite/) itself recognised this singularity and **blessed her sword with a divine power** capable of fighting the forces of evil with formidable efficiency.

Blanche became **guardian and protector**, devoting her life to order and justice, always seeking to build an organised and good world despite the chaos of her origins.

## The brother she cannot understand

But her greatest torment came from her brother, **the Heir of the Void** — who possessed the demonic share she had never received, and who used it to sow chaos and laugh at the suffering of gods and humans.

She could not understand him — him who played with destinies as a child plays with toys, him who laughed at the disorder he created.

## The eternal war of halves

This incomprehension turned into **permanent conflict** between them. Two halves of the same bloodline, become enemies. **Order against chaos.**

Blanche fought with rage what she saw as the embodiment of pure cruelty — unaware that, perhaps, somewhere, her brother was as lost as she was certain.

And so Rose's daughter became **a solitary warrior** — fighting not only the external powers of evil, but also the darkness represented by **the blood of her own father, flowing through the veins of the brother she both loved and despised**.
